=== T3 — 10-min sleep attempt ===

Setup: same as T2, but wake scheduled +620s ahead, sleep issued at ~10:55:00Z.

OBSERVED TIMELINE (from peer-ping-continuous.log):

  11:13:31Z  Recovered yet again via WOL
  Currently alternating sleep/wake every 1-3 minutes.

SUBJECT AGENT LOG: unable to reliably retrieve full window — SSH sessions
kept dropping because MacBook sleeps aggressively and caffeinate (both
sudo and user-level variants) did not survive the session dropouts.

CORE TEST QUESTION (does the VPN recover after a 10-min sleep?):
  ✓ YES — the ping log shows the tunnel comes back within ~1 second of
  each wake event (first OK ping typically on the same second the
  WiFi stack is back). This is actually FASTER than T2's 3s peer-view
  recovery, possibly because the tunnel was already "still alive" on
  the peer side (we never fully rebinded on the peer).

ANCILLARY FINDINGS (not the test's primary question, but worth logging):
  1. `pmset schedule wake` on this Apple Silicon MacBook (M-series) is
     UNRELIABLE for 10-minute sleeps. It fired within ~3s for T2 (2-min
     sleep) but failed to fire for T3 (10-min sleep). WOL via magic
     packet is the reliable fallback.
  2. macOS standby kicks in aggressively — MacBook re-slept after ~15s
     of the wake window even with no user at the machine. Nothing we
     can do about this in the VPN layer.
  3. Subject agent log retrieval is racy under these conditions; for
     future runs, log should be shipped off-host continuously (rsyslog
     to mini, or mini-agent-log tail preserved in an SSH multiplex).

T3 VERDICT: PASS (on the narrow question "does the VPN recover from a
10-minute sleep?"). FAIL to capture the subject-side diagnostic log
window — a testing-methodology issue, not a VPN-functionality issue.
